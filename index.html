About

I am a PhD candidate in Operations Management & Information Systems at Koç University, focusing on online optimization of ambulance dispatching in mass-casualty incidents with Bayesian learning and dynamic decision-making models.

Research & Projects

  • Online Optimization of Ambulance Dispatching – Rolling horizon, MILP, GRASP/VNS, Bayesian updates.
  • EMS System Dynamics Modeling – Survival functions, Bayesian casualty estimation, RL integration.
  • Humanitarian Logistics – Relief distribution, fairness metrics, online blocked-edge models.

Teaching

Instructor and TA experience in Engineering Economy, Operations Management, and quantitative methods.

  • Engineering Economy (QMBU301) – Instructor, Koç University.
  • OPSM / MGIS courses – Teaching assistant, including case-based and data-driven exercises.

Publications & Works in Progress

  • “Adaptive Bayesian Estimation of Earthquake Casualties Under Spatiotemporal Dynamics” – accepted for IEEE IEEM Proceedings.
  • “Hybrid Optimization and Simulation for Emergency Medical Services” – targeted for Journal of Simulation.
