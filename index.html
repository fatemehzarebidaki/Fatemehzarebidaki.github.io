Welcome

I am a PhD candidate in Operations Management & Information Systems at Koç University Graduate School of Business (Istanbul). My research focuses on online optimization, Bayesian learning, and decision support for Emergency Medical Services (EMS) and humanitarian logistics.

I develop mixed-integer optimization models, rolling-horizon heuristics, and simulation-based approaches to improve ambulance dispatching and casualty transportation decisions under uncertainty in mass-casualty incidents.

Research Highlights

  • Online optimization of ambulance dispatching with Bayesian updates.
  • Rolling-horizon MILP models with GRASP/VNS heuristics.
  • System dynamics and simulation for EMS performance evaluation.
  • Fairness and equity in humanitarian logistics and relief distribution.

See more details on the Research and Publications pages.
